Cash tracking is where leakage happens

Captain cash (100102) must reconcile to Counter cash (100101) every shift. The variance between Captain POS total and physical cash handover is your first leakage checkpoint. Your existing cashier shift settlement system tracks this — the captain_cash_variance field.

ALL UPI currently goes through Razorpay = 2.36% on every UPI payment

Both Captain UPI (HC01) and Counter UPI (RPY1) are Razorpay QR codes — both charge 2% + GST = 2.36%. The only difference is tracking: Captain UPI lands in account 100207, Counter UPI in 100206. Consider setting up a direct bank UPI QR (0% cost) for high-value dine-in to eliminate this fee. On Rs 5L/month UPI revenue, you'd save Rs 11,800/month.

Where Every Rupee Ultimately Sits

CASH Track Points

Captain Cash (100102) → settled to → Counter Cash (100101) → deposited to → HDFC Bank (100201)

Reconciliation checkpoints:

1. Captain POS total vs physical cash handover (shift end)

2. Counter expected vs actual cash count (shift end)

3. Cash deposited to bank vs counter closing balance

Fee: 0% — but highest risk of leakage

UPI Two Paths

Captain UPI (100207) — direct bank QR, 0% fee, instant

Razorpay UPI (100206) — via Razorpay, 2.36% fee, T+2

Reconciliation:

1. Match Captain UPI (Odoo) vs bank statement UPI credits

2. Match Razorpay dashboard settlements vs Odoo RPY1 journal

Always prefer Captain UPI (0%) over Razorpay UPI (2.36%)

Cost Layers of Each Sale — What's Revenue vs External Budget

Important Distinction: Direct Cost of Sale vs External Budget

Direct Cost of Sale = commission, payment fees, GST on fees — deducted from the order revenue itself.
External Budget = Meta ad spend, EazyDiner 10% discount, marketing costs — these are separate marketing expenses, NOT deducted from order revenue. They appear in the P&L under Marketing (Section 5), not under Revenue.
