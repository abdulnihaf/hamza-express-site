What is "Gross Sales"? This is critical.

Gross Sales = Your menu price on Zomato + GST (5%) + Packaging charge (if customer-facing) MINUS any discounts YOU fund on the platform. Since Zomato delivers (their rider), delivery charges are NOT deducted. So Zomato's 18% applies to almost the full order value.

Hidden penalty costs that can destroy your margins

Customer Complaint Refund

50% of refund amount charged to you. If Zomato refunds Rs 350 to customer, you lose Rs 175. Applies after first 2 weeks.

Order Rejection Penalty

If weekly rejections > 0.5% of orders: 10% of rejected order value. If > 2%: 25%. Never reject unless absolutely necessary.

Price Disparity

3x the price difference per order. If your biryani is Rs 250 on Zomato but Rs 220 dine-in, they charge 3 x Rs 30 = Rs 90 PER ORDER until you fix it.

Discouraged Practices

Sending flyers/discounts to Zomato customers to divert them: Up to Rs 1,00,000 per contravention.

TCS is NOT a cost — it's a tax advance

The 1% TCS (Tax Collected at Source) is deducted by Swiggy and deposited with the government on your behalf. You claim it back when filing your income tax return. So the real cost difference between Swiggy and Zomato is only about 0.19% (Collection Fee 2% vs Payment Fee 1.84%).

Zomato Dining is your best aggregator deal

At 7-8% effective cost, Zomato Dining is 3x cheaper than Zomato Delivery (23.4%). If a customer is in the restaurant and wants to pay via Zomato app for their rewards — that's fine, it only costs you 8%. Encourage dine-in customers who discover you on Zomato.

If a customer books a paid table reservation and doesn't show up, the entire forfeited cover charge goes to Zomato. You get nothing. This applies to non-offer, offer-based, and peak-hour reservations. For 20 years (till 2046).

Golden Rules for Profitable Selling

RULE 1: Channel Priority

Direct > Dining > Delivery

Always push dine-in and WhatsApp first. Zomato Dining second. Delivery aggregators last. The margin difference is 3-4x.

RULE 2: Price Parity is Mandatory

3x Penalty

Both Zomato and Swiggy contractually require equal or lower prices on platform vs dine-in. Zomato charges 3x the price difference per order. Never price differently.

RULE 3: Never Reject Orders

< 0.5%

Keep weekly order rejection below 0.5%. Above that, Zomato charges 10% of rejected order values. Above 2%, it's 25%. Train kitchen to accept everything.

RULE 4: Complaint Refunds Cost You 50%

50%

If Zomato refunds a customer Rs 350, they take Rs 175 from you. Quality control and correct packaging directly impact your bottom line.

RULE 5: Push Combos on Low-AOV Channels

Rs 150+

Below Rs 150, delivery orders are barely profitable after COGS + packaging + labor. Design combos that push average order value above Rs 200 on aggregators.

RULE 6: Recover Drops

72h

WhatsApp leads that don't convert in 24h get 3 follow-ups over 72h. Mumtaz recovers dine-in drops by call. Every recovered order is a direct-channel order (2.36% vs 24%).
