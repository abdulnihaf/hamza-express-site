The Lineage

One family. One street.
Four generations cooking.

Most restaurants in Shivajinagar are younger than our youngest cook. We are not. We've been on this stretch of H.K.P. Road since the year of the Spanish Flu, and we are still here.

The first kitchen. Our great-grandfather sets up a small Dakhni biryani counter on H.K.P. Road, in what was then a market town outside Bangalore Cantonment. The recipes come down the family from Hyderabad.

From the Kitchen

The dishes you'll be served.

Mutton Biryani

The 1918 recipe

Dum-cooked in sealed handi. Long-grain basmati layered with mutton that's been marinated overnight, ghee, fried onions, and our family's own spice blend. The dish that built our reputation.

Brain Dry

The 1950s addition

Mutton brain, slow-cooked dry with green chillies, ginger, and curry leaves. Most cafés in Bangalore can't make this. We've been making it since the second generation. Reserved for serious eaters.

Chicken Kabab

From the coal tandoor

Hand-minced chicken, family masala, threaded onto skewers and grilled over real coal — not gas, not electric. The smoke is part of the flavor. Our top revenue dish, and it shows.

Ghee Rice

The unsung hero

Pure ghee, basmati, whole spices. Simple in the way only old kitchens can do simple. Outsells biryani by a small margin — Bangalore knows what it's looking for.

Tandoori Chicken

Charred, never dry

Yogurt-marinated 24 hours. Pulled out of the tandoor exactly when the skin chars and the meat is still juicy. A test of the kitchen — we pass it daily.

Bheja Fry

The Hamza signature

Mutton brain, tempered with green chillies, onions, and the family masala. Bangalore knows us for this — it's the dish on every regular's order. There's a reason our IG bio leads with it: it's the line you can't fake.

Hamza Special

Honest Answers

Questions you're likely to have.

How is this different from every other "free meal for a reel" deal?

Three things. First, we are a 108-year-old family business — not a chain doing influencer marketing for the third time this month. Second, the offer is pre-defined and transparent — no haggling, no mid-meal surprises. Third, we host one creator per window. You eat with our family, not in a queue with five other influencers.

Can I bring guests?

Cover count is the size of your party. Nano (1K–5K) = 1 cover, you alone. Micro (5K–15K) = 2 covers, you + 1 guest. Mid-Micro (15K–30K) = 3 covers. Upper-Micro through Edge-Macro = 4 covers. Macro (250K+) = 6 covers. The full breakdown is in each tier's invitation card above.

What if my engagement rate is low?

We look at active engagement, not just follower count. Below 0.5% ER, we auto-decline — that protects both sides. From 30K+ followers, we expect ER ≥ 1.5% for instant approval; otherwise we review manually within 24h. Inflated counts won't get past us.

Is there cash, or just the meal?

Tiers T1 through T4 are barter — meal, hosting, slot. From T5 (60K+ followers) onwards, we add cash on top of the meal: ₹500 at T5, ₹3,000 at T6, ₹8,000 at T7. Each tier card above shows the exact figure. For pure-cash collabs at T7+, we'll have a real conversation.

How soon can I come?

If you fall in T1–T4 with passing engagement, you're auto-confirmed the moment you submit — pick any slot in the next 21 days, you're booked. T5+ are reviewed by us personally within 24 hours. Eid (May 27) slots fill fastest. Don't wait.

What if I have YouTube or a newsletter too?

Tell us in the application. The auto-tier is computed from Instagram followers, but cross-platform reach gets noted and helps us host you better. Multi-platform creators sometimes get bumped a tier on manual review.

What happens after I post?

Nothing pushy. We don't message you weekly, don't ask for analytics screenshots, don't bug you. We watch the post organically. If we love it, we'll reach out about a return visit at a higher tier — and then yes, with cash.

Why are you doing this now?

Because Bangalore has loud opinions about biryani, and the loudest opinions usually come from the newest restaurants. We've been in Shivajinagar since 1918. We've just facelifted the room. We'd like the city to know we're still here, still cooking, and still better than the chain next door. Honest reels from creators we trust — that's how we're telling that story.
